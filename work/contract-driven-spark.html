Contract Driven Data Platform with Spark

A batch platform designed around predictable schemas, layered modeling, and quality gates so multi-source data stays safe to use for analytics.

Spark Data Contracts Validation Rules Incremental Processing

Problem

When multiple teams publish data, tables drift. Columns change, meanings change, and downstream models quietly break. Analytics becomes fragile and people stop trusting outputs.

Approach

I structured the pipeline around contracts (expected schema + business meaning), validation rules at ingestion, and staged layers that make transformations predictable.

What “contract-driven” means here

Contract = schema + constraints + meaning

Examples:
- Required fields must exist (and be non-null)
- Primary identifiers must be unique
- Value ranges must be valid (no negative quantities)
- Event time must be parseable (and within reasonable bounds)

Incremental + maintainable by design

Instead of doing full reloads, I added incremental processing logic where it made sense, so jobs remain efficient as data volumes grow. The design goal was not just “it runs”, but “it stays understandable six months later.”

Outcome

A safer foundation for analytics: incoming data is checked early, transformations stay structured, and issues are caught before they land in dashboards.
