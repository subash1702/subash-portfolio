Dual-Layer E-Commerce Data Platform

I built a platform that treats streaming metrics and batch reporting as two different jobs with two different failure modes — then designed each layer to be reliable on its own and consistent together.

Why I built this

Most real-time dashboards look fast until real data shows up: events arrive late, ordering breaks, and the numbers quietly drift. At the same time, finance and reporting teams need stable batch datasets that reconcile cleanly.

The goal was to keep two truths aligned — operational metrics that update quickly, and reporting data that stays auditable.

Layer 1: real-time event processing

The streaming layer computes sales velocity and inventory triggers using windowed aggregates. The key constraint was correctness under chaos: late events, duplicates, and out-of-order data should not corrupt metrics.

  • Sliding and tumbling windows for sales velocity and inventory triggers
  • Handling out-of-order and late events without corrupting aggregates
  • Alerts for critical stock fluctuations and sudden revenue spikes

Layer 2: reliable batch analytics

The batch layer produces stable datasets for reporting. Runs are idempotent, transformations are versioned in dbt, and data quality checks validate schema and business logic before anything is served.

  • Daily ingestion into a warehouse-friendly model
  • dbt transformations to clean and standardize raw tables
  • Idempotent retries to prevent duplication
  • DQ checks for schema + business rules

What I’d improve next

Add schema contracts at ingestion (registry + tests) and anomaly checks for null spikes and distribution drift.
